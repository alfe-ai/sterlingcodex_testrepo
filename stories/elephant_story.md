The Elephant Who Remembered Rain

In a dusty savanna, an old elephant named Kito carried memories as heavy as his tusks. He remembered every rain that had fallen in a dozen seasons: the sounds, the smells, the way the grass drank the sky and grew tall enough to tickle his trunk.

One dry season, a tiny mouse named Luma asked, "Why do you keep so many memories?" Kito lowered his head and whispered, "Memories are rain that never leaves the earth. When the clouds forget to come, I plant the old rains in my heart and still feel the coolness in the air."
